Directions

Plattsburgh is located close to the Canadian border, directly bordering Vermont on Lake Champlain. It is about 3 hours' drive north of Albany NY, and about 1.5 hours' drive south of Montreal.

Traveling to Plattsburgh

By car

We recommend traveling to Plattsburgh by car if coming from anywhere in the northeast, or by plane from anywhere else. There is no public transit in Plattsburgh.

If you have extra room in your car for carpooling or are looking for a ride in a carpool, please fill out this form by July 15.

By plane

Plattsburgh has an airport which only receives flights from Boston, Washington DC (Dulles), Myrtle Beach, and several cities in Florida.

The nearest major airport to Plattsburgh is in Montreal, and this is likely the best airport to fly into when traveling to Plattsburgh. However, after flying into Montreal you will need to drive to Plattsburgh, or travel into downtown Montreal to catch a bus or train.

It is also possible to fly into Burlington VT, Albany NY, or New York City and then drive to Plattsburgh.

By bus

There are Greyhound (and Adirondack Trailways) buses which run several times a day between New York City, Albany and Montreal that stop in Plattsburgh. There are also some buses that arrive from other parts of upstate NY and Vermont.

Please be advised that the bus station in Plattsburgh is on the edge of the city and you will need a taxi or someone to pick you up, and that it is not a real bus station, it's a gas station.

By train

There is an Amtrak train which runs once per day between New York City, Albany and Montreal that stops in Plattsburgh. This train is very slow compared to a car or bus.

The train station is located in downtown Plattsburgh, close to our wedding venue but not very close to any hotels.

Travel within Plattsburgh

Please be aware that there is no public transit in Plattsburgh!
